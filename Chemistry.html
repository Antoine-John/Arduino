Chemistry

Topic 1

States of matter and their particles

Solid, liquids and gases

Solids - they have a fixed shape and a fixed volume. It does not flow. In a solid, the particles are in a fixed pattern or lattice. Strong forces hold them together.

Liquids - they do not have a fixed shape but have a fixed volume. It can flow easily. In a liquid, the particles can move about and slide past each other. The forces holding them together is weaker than in a solid.

Gases - gasses o not have a fixed shape or volume. It spreads out to fill it's container. In a gas, the particles are very far apart. The particles move about very quickly.There are almost no forces holding them together.


Changing state

Melting - When solid is heated, the particles get more energy and vibrate more. This causes the solid to expand.

Evaporating - When a liquid is heated, its teperature rises, it's particles get more energy and move faster. the particles bumb together more eften making the liquid expand. The particles get enough energy to break away to form a gas. NOTE : Liquids do not need to be boiling to evaporate.

The oposite change happens whaen a gas or liquid is cooled.

Diffusion of gasses

Rate of diffusion depends of two things : Mass of the particles and the temperature. The lower the mass of it's particles, the faster a gas will difuse, the higher the mass, the slower a gas will diffuse. The higher the temperature the faster a gas will diffuse.

Mixtures, solutions, and solvents

Mixtures - mixtures contain more than one substance. The substances are mixed together but not chemically combined.
Solutions - solutions are a mixture obtained when a solute is dissolved in a solvent.For instance, when sugar dissolves in water. sugar is the solute and water is the solvent. In order for a mixture to be called a solution, the solution needs to be liquid and translucent.

Pure substances and impurities

Pure substances have no other substances mixed with it. For instance, tap water is impure as it contains many other different substances such as minerals. Distilled water is pure as it only contains one type of molecule, H2O.

How can you tell if a substance is pure?

To test wether a substance is pure, you can test it's boiling and melting points. A pure substance has a definite, sharp, melting point and boiling point.
